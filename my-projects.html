    1.  Automation of Vertical Blanking Interval

    The project was my six month internship at Broad India Pvt Ltd at Bangalore. The Project was complete under the mentorship of Mr. Gurunath Kulkarni and under the supervision of Mr. Ravi Bhaskaran. you can read more about  Vertical Blanking Interval here. The project involved hours of geeky experimentation with Gage Card and Oscilloscopes. Once the signals were digitally captured it was stored in MATLAB . I made an algorithm to recognize the specific vsync and Hsyncs and then wrote separate algorithms for each kind of VBI. I cant share all the codes because of Broadcom's propriety guidelines. Still, I had prepared a report to beevaluated and  submitted in my university. I have described  VBI SIgnals, Gage Capture Card , My first line detection algorithm and the code for GUI in PERL.     2. Intelligent Cardiac Monitoring System

    The paper has been published in International Journal of Online Engineering (ISSN: 1861-2121) in its Virtual Instrumentation issue Vol-7 ,No3(2011). paper at online journal IJOE .You can read the full text by downloading our paper here   Intelligent Cardiac Monitoring.pdf (820247)

    The project developed a cheap and portable device for heart patients to keep track of heart rhythm. The project is based on Atmega 32 microcontroller, hardware filters & Programmable Systems on Chip coupled with microphone to acquire the heart sound signals. In case of anomaly, the device is able to warn the user to get required medical help. It also saves the data for future diagnosis. The paper published also contains FFT analysis of normal heart signals and how the abnormal data will show a shift in the frequency. A databse of various diseases in terms of frequancy was recommended for subsequent group of students who took the project. ; online journal IJOE .You can read the full text by downloading our paper here

    3. Reverse Solar Refrigeration

     It has been developed with the objective to help deliver vaccines to regions of the world without electricity or with inadequate electrical supply. A combined cycle of heating and cooling was envisioned through adsorption on charcal with the help of mehanol working directly on solar panels, hence, bypassing the use of conventional lead batteries and kerosene refrigerators.The proposed prototype is a versatile refrigeration technology that is environment-friendly, battery-free, technologically-reliable and affordable. The project won second prize in the BITS Pilani Technical Meet-  APOGEE 2008. It then went on to be selected in the first round of KVPY(Kishore Vaigyanik Protsahan Yojana) in the Stream SB( for undergraduates) category. You can read the full report here  kvpy report1.doc. Also in the paper form   solar hybrid.doc
